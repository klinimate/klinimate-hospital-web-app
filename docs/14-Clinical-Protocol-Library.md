# Klinimate Clinical Protocol Library

Version: 2.0

Status: Active

Owner: Dr. Avdhut Kulkarni (Founder & Director)

---

# Purpose

The Klinimate Clinical Protocol Library is the official evidence-informed clinical knowledge repository used by the Klinimate Platform.

It provides standardized clinical pathways that support healthcare professionals across every healthcare setting while preserving clinician autonomy.

The Clinical Protocol Library is part of the **Knowledge Base** and serves as a trusted reference for the Klinimate Intelligence Engine.

---

# Objectives

The Clinical Protocol Library aims to:

- Standardize patient assessment.
- Reduce variation in clinical practice.
- Improve patient safety.
- Support junior clinicians.
- Assist experienced clinicians.
- Reduce missed clinical deterioration.
- Improve documentation quality.
- Support AI-assisted clinical workflows.

---

# Knowledge Base Philosophy

The Clinical Protocol Library provides **clinical knowledge**.

It does **not** perform reasoning.

The Klinimate Intelligence Engine interprets patient information and references the appropriate protocols when generating recommendations.

Knowledge and reasoning remain separate.

---

# Guiding Principles

Every protocol should:

- Follow current evidence-based practice.
- Be practical and clinically relevant.
- Be mobile-friendly.
- Minimize unnecessary documentation.
- Support—not replace—clinical judgement.
- Be version-controlled.
- Be regularly reviewed.

---

# Standard Protocol Structure

Every protocol should follow the same format.

## 1. Overview

- Definition
- Clinical Importance
- Common Causes
- Risk Factors

---

## 2. Clinical Assessment

- Presenting Symptoms
- Relevant History
- Physical Examination
- Red Flags

---

## 3. Recommended Investigations

- Laboratory Tests
- Imaging
- Point-of-Care Tests
- Additional Investigations (when appropriate)

---

## 4. Initial Management

- Immediate Priorities
- Supportive Care
- Monitoring
- Time-Critical Interventions

Treatment recommendations should remain evidence-informed and non-prescriptive.

---

## 5. Monitoring

Recommended monitoring includes:

- Clinical Reassessment
- Vital Signs
- Intake & Output (when appropriate)
- Laboratory Monitoring
- Response to Treatment

---

## 6. Escalation Criteria

Patients should be categorized as:

- Stable
- Needs Review
- Urgent
- Critical

Each level should define appropriate clinical actions.

---

## 7. Specialist Support

Indications for requesting Klinimate Support.

Examples include:

- Intensivist
- Physician
- Cardiologist
- Pulmonologist
- Neurologist
- Nephrologist
- Endocrinologist
- Surgeon
- Other Specialists

---

## 8. Disposition

Possible outcomes include:

- Continue Local Care
- Admission
- ICU Care
- Transfer
- Home Care
- Rehabilitation
- Follow-up
- Discharge

---

## 9. Clinical Documentation

Protocols should support documentation of:

- Medical Notes
- Nursing Notes
- Vitals
- Investigations
- Medications
- Procedures
- Patient Intelligence
- Specialist Recommendations

---

# Integration with Klinimate Intelligence

The Klinimate Intelligence Engine uses the Clinical Protocol Library as a trusted clinical reference.

When appropriate, the AI may:

- Suggest relevant protocols.
- Highlight important clinical red flags.
- Recommend investigations.
- Recommend monitoring priorities.
- Recommend specialist support.
- Improve Patient Intelligence.

The AI never replaces clinician judgement.

- Transparent
