Version: 2.0

# Purpose

The Klinimate Intelligence Engine (KIE) is the core AI engine that powers the Klinimate Platform.

It continuously analyzes structured and unstructured clinical information to generate Patient Intelligence, assist clinical decision-making, reduce documentation burden, and coordinate specialist-supported care throughout the patient's healthcare journey.

KIE supports every healthcare setting while keeping clinicians in complete control of patient care.

---

# Intelligence Philosophy

Klinimate Intelligence should feel like an experienced clinical colleague who is always available.

The engine should:

- Reduce documentation burden
- Reduce clinician workload
- Improve patient safety
- Standardize clinical documentation
- Improve communication between care teams
- Detect clinical deterioration early
- Support evidence-informed decision-making
- Never interrupt clinical workflow
- Work seamlessly on mobile devices

KIE assists clinicians.

It never replaces clinical judgement.

# Intelligent Clinical Assessment

Rather than displaying long forms, KIE asks only clinically relevant questions.

Examples

### Breathlessness

- Oxygen requirement?
- Chest X-ray available?
- ABG available?

### Chest Pain

- ECG performed?
- Troponin available?

### Fever

- Duration?
- Suspected source?
- Blood cultures sent?

### Stroke

- Time of onset?
- CT Brain completed?

The objective is to collect maximum clinical information with minimum effort.

# Patient Intelligence

Patient Intelligence is the primary output generated by KIE.

It continuously updates throughout the patient's episode of care.

It includes:

- AI Clinical Summary
- Current Clinical Status
- Clinical Priorities
- Suggested Differential Diagnoses
- Suggested Investigations
- Treatment Considerations
- Clinical Alerts
- Confidence Level
- Follow-up Recommendations

---

# Clinical Status

Patient Intelligence classifies the patient's current condition as:

- Stable
- Needs Review
- Urgent
- Critical

This status updates automatically whenever new clinical information becomes available.

---

# Suggested Differential Diagnoses

KIE suggests evidence-informed differential diagnoses ranked by probability.

These suggestions assist clinicians and do not establish a final diagnosis.

---

# Suggested Investigations

KIE recommends clinically relevant investigations based on the patient's presentation.

Doctors may always modify or manually add investigations.

---

# Treatment Considerations

KIE provides high-level evidence-informed treatment considerations.

Examples include:

- Oxygen therapy consideration
- Fluid assessment
- Infection evaluation
- Glycaemic optimization
- DVT prophylaxis review

KIE never independently prescribes medications.

---

# Clinical Alerts

KIE generates intelligent alerts when clinically significant events occur.

Examples include:

- Clinical deterioration
- Abnormal vital trends
- Oliguria
- Persistent hypoxia
- New abnormal investigation
- Missed follow-up
- Critical laboratory result

Alerts are designed to reduce alert fatigue by displaying only clinically meaningful notifications.

---

# Confidence Level

KIE continuously evaluates the completeness of available clinical information.

Levels:

- High
- Moderate
- Low

If confidence is Moderate or Low, KIE requests only the missing information required to improve clinical assessment.

---

# Klinimate Support Recommendation

When appropriate, KIE recommends requesting Klinimate Support.

Possible specialties include:

- Intensivist
- Physician
- Cardiologist
- Pulmonologist
- Neurologist
- Nephrologist
- Endocrinologist
- Infectious Disease Specialist
- Other Specialists

The final decision always remains with the treating healthcare professional.

# Continuous Intelligence

Patient Intelligence continuously updates whenever new information is added.

Examples include:

- Medical Notes
- Nursing Notes
- Vitals

- Medication Administration
- Investigation Results
- Clinical Documents
- Medication Changes
- Consultant Reviews
- Virtual Specialist Advice

Patient Intelligence always reflects the most recent clinical information.

---

# Patient Intelligence Finalization

Before discharge, the treating clinician reviews the Patient Intelligence Summary.

The clinician may:

- Edit the AI-generated summary
- Modify the clinical outcome
- Update the follow-up plan
- Finalize the summary

Once finalized, Patient Intelligence becomes the source for generating:

- Discharge Summary
- Referral Summary
- Billing Support Summary
- Insurance Support Summary

# Safety Principles

KIE:

- Never replaces clinician judgement
- Never functions autonomously
- Clearly separates AI suggestions from clinician decisions
- Encourages escalation when uncertainty is high
- Maintains complete auditability
- Requires clinician review before finalization
