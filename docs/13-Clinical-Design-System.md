Status: Active

# Purpose

This document defines the official clinical design standards for the Klinimate Platform.

It serves as the single source of truth for:

- Clinical workflows
- Clinical documentation
- Patient Intelligence
- Patient Timeline
- Clinical collaboration
- Virtual specialist workflow
- Clinical safety
- Clinical user experience

Every clinical module within the Klinimate ecosystem must follow this design system.

---

# Clinical Philosophy

Klinimate assists healthcare professionals—it never replaces them.

The platform should:

- Simplify clinical documentation.
- Standardize patient assessment.
- Reduce documentation burden.
- Improve communication.
- Detect clinical deterioration early.
- Support evidence-informed decision-making.
- Enable seamless collaboration.
- Keep clinicians in control.

Final responsibility always remains with the treating healthcare professional.

# Patient Intelligence

Patient Intelligence is the primary clinical overview within Klinimate.

It continuously analyses:

- Medical Notes
- Nursing Notes
- Vitals
- Intake & Output
- Investigations
- Medications
- Procedures
- Clinical Documents
- Patient Progress
- Specialist Recommendations

It generates:

- AI Clinical Summary
- Current Clinical Status
- Clinical Priorities
- Suggested Differential Diagnoses
- Suggested Investigations
- Treatment Considerations
- Clinical Alerts
- Confidence Level
- Suggested Next Steps

Patient Intelligence is advisory.

Clinicians always make the final decisions.

# Clinical Safety

Klinimate must:

- Never replace clinician judgement.
- Clearly distinguish AI-generated information from clinician documentation.
- Maintain a complete audit trail.
- Preserve historical records.
- Encourage escalation when appropriate.

# Patient Intelligence Summary

At the end of an Episode of Care, Patient Intelligence generates a comprehensive summary.

The summary supports:

- Clinical Handover
- Discharge Summary
- Referral Summary
- Insurance Documentation
- Billing Support
- Home Care Planning
- Rehabilitation Planning

The summary must be:

- Reviewed
- Edited if required
- Finalized by the treating clinician

Only finalized summaries become part of the permanent clinical record.
